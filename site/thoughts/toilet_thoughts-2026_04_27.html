Toilet Thought

27/04/2026 — "A thought I had whilst taking a morning dump"

Technology’s existence should only be justified to make our life easier, and it certainly has. The world has become much much smaller than it used to be. It gave us the oppotunity to know more and to reach many more people.

But how do we continue to justify it when it stops making it easier and actually starts to make things more difficult or take longer. Can we still say that we are in control of it.

Yes, we can turn it off whenever we want to and we can choose to leave it at home when going out. But can we actually? How many people would expect you to stay connected? Is there any way you could meet people that you are close with without it? Then, how in control are we really?

None of this was an individual’s decision, it was a collective one. One without a clear decision or vote but with gradual adoption over time. Driven by the promise of convenience. Driven by the justification of making life easier.

Then, we reach that same question again...
When it stops making it easier and actually starts to make things more difficult or take longer, how do we continue to justify it?
