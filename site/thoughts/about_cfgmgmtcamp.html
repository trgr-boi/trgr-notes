about_cfgmgmtcamp

We had a conference at school called config management camp. A conference where people from all over the world come ans speak about everying related to configuration of server/systems. There were so many more taks than that I could attend to. So I mainly choose ones about automation. I choose multiple ones about Ansible and linux in general, and the last day I went to a workshop about Fedora Bluefin to declaratively configure a system in a github repo that automaticaly creates a boot image. It was given by James Freeman. I saw almost all of his talks and really liked them. He was vert carefull and made clear that most of what he was talking about was very opinionated (like everything in Linux) and that his was not the default or best option at all. But always gave strong reasons about why he personaly chose these options.

Talking with this many pasionate and skilled people really motivates me to learn more ans set clearer goals for myself to improve. Even though there was a lot I disagreed with, mainly the AI praisers, it still learned me to look for things I could use. Take for example the aforementioned workshop about Bluefin. I don't think I will use this. I get the appeal about it for sure and it is very cool how it works, and that this is even possible. But for me personally, I would like to stick to the normal fedora. Mainly because I use i3wm and I have yet to find a immutable os (of this type) that has a i3wm spin. But it made me think about the full automation of setting up a workstation differently and showed some things that you have to think about that I didn't before.

Overall, it was a very motivating experience and I'm happy that I went. Will be doing this next year as well! I will also make it a long-term goal to give my own talk there as well.
