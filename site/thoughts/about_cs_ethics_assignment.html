I had an assignment for school where we need to give a argumentative presentation about a topic chosen from a list. I chose the subject "Should computer science curricula mandate ethics courses to prevent future tech harms?".

Ethics is something that I have always seen as one of the most important subject of society. It makes sure that decisions are not made in act of selfishness but for the greater good. This motivated me to do a lot of research around the topic. We only needed 3 credible sources, but I had already collected some about 'general ethics' over the years and found many more about ethics in IT. So call me over-prepared :)

One of the sources I found was the Code of Ethics by ACM. This gives a good rule set about what ethics mean for IT and how you can implement them. Although this is a powerful code, if it is not actively mandated to learn about this, than it will never be effective. That is why I think that every educational facility should teach a ethics subject.

We have the issue in IT that everything is made by technical people, who haven't had any ethical education. This leads to great innovation but innovation that does not keep the greater good in consideration. Look at the AI-boom. It entered the market before ant ethics where put into question. Whilst is was and still is a great tool, it is designed to make you dependent on it. And only after a while where the companies that developed this tool forced to be more open about how correct it is. Now they need to "ChatGPT can make mistakes" and "Check the answers for correctness", But they were in the beginning selling this as 'A tool that knows everything and can do everything better than human'.

After this chapter, I felt motivated to start "practicing" ethics again. To actively think about all of this again, and to write it down.

This is all I wanted to say for now.
