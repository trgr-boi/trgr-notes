Hyperfixation

Now Playing: Water Planets - Deepspace

14/10/2025

I can never be "just" interested in something. I always go overboard with interest until I burn out. I make it my personality almost.
I've stopped being super open about my fixations. Only with the people close to me.

For a year now, I have built my own digital world. Most of what I use, I host myself. This journey started to escape from the grasp of big corporations and a genuine interest in privacy, but quickly became a hyper fixation.
I still genuinely like the process of doing this, and by doing this, I am progressing a skill that I need for both school and future work.

But when is too much?

Is it wrong to change old habits so they follow my new belief system? Why is privacy and not wanting to go along with a system, that I morally don't agree with, even important?
Idk...
It is just a battle i have chosen in a way. i feel like i always have to pick a battle, but i think everyone does. at least most of the people around me.
Will it by the next "upgrade" — or downgrade, depending how you look at it — be enough? Or will there be a new thing to want to change, or to buy?

So maybe is every hyper fixations just another battle. A battle that takes a lot of time and energy, only to be dropped when the fixation burns out. A battle that actually leans closer an ever-evolving form of mindlessly-mindful consumption. Just to in some form of another feel like I have a better opinion or more knowledge. To feel better than someone.
Even though I am just a no-life in a lot of ways.

Does it become too much when it starts taking away from the part of life that truly matters?
Wanting to be less dependant on attention sucking companies — out of an annoyance of everybody getting more and more sucked in. Only to spend hours and hours behind a screen at home so I can be more "present" than the person on the bus who doesn't look away even ones when watching reels.

I don’t really know where I want to go with this. These are just some questions that I regularly ask myself.
